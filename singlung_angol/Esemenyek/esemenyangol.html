COPD World Day

2018 November

In November 2018, on the occasion of the COPD World Day, a choir assembled from the patients of three pulmonary hospitals performed in the main hall of the Benczur Hotel. The patients were practicing for a week with their art teachers in the National Korányi Pulmonology Institute, in the Mátra State Medical Institute and the Pulmonary Hospital of Farkasgyepü. After a common rehearsal day, they took the stage at the Conference of World COPD Day as the end of the conference.
They sang the songs: Tamás Cseh - Géza Bereményi: Prayer and
Gábor Presser - Dusán Sztevanoity: If the angels are coming
The art teachers performed Rachmaninov's wonderful Bogoroditse Dievo, Raduisia.

Presentation of the book of Nurses in 2018

2018

‘In April, a touching volume of portraits was published by the ‘Légzéssel a Lélekért’ Foundation of the respiratory rehabilitation department of Pulmonary Hospital of Törökbálint. Performance with the Festival Orchestra

2021 October

The pandemic did not leave the choir untouched either. We tried to connect and practice on the Internet. A chance meeting resulted in a special invitation. The Choir had the honor of being invited by Iván Fischer on the stage of the MÜPA where were able to perform at one of the first concerts of a Festival Orchestra after opening. The Choir performed cheerful medieval student songs accompanied by a special organ pipe. Iván Fischer assured the Choir and the SingLung method of his recognition and sincere support. A film was also made with their help. It was a great experience to sing in front of such a numerous and understanding audience.

Aria Libera

2022 July

In July 2022, the SingLung Choir undertook a 50-minute program for the first time, where already the movement and the fine coordination of the body's muscles also played a role. György Phillip wrote this in the introduction:
‘... the Aria Libera, the open air also means the balance of artistic creation in which progress and tradition are present at the same time. Opposite truths are present at the same time in the pursuit of balance in human existence. One does not exist without the other one; pieces of these contrasts together give the whole. The basic idea of the presentation was given by the image, what eventually became the logo of our method: the blossomed lung. If we imagine a lung and the branches or roots of a tree next to each other, we can discover surprising similarities. The tree is the lungs of the Earth. We breathe in what the Earth breathes out and what we breathe out, the Earth inhales it. Together we are the Nature...’
