About the Breathing for the Soul Foundation

The idea of the Breathing for the Soul Foundation arose in 2017 at the Respiratory Rehabilitation Department of the Lung Hospital of Törökbálint, where we thought that we should look for common creative activities and opportunities for togetherness of the patients and healers, and during this their physical and mental health as well as their community will be strengthened for better recovery.

Our first activity was the organization of the professional ‘Move with oxygen’ congress, where using the ‘world cafe’ method, doctors and nurses thought together the questions arising during the care of chronic patients applying the latest scientific and technical achievements. The book titled Nurses published by the Foundation and Ob Ovo was presented here and the patients' choir performed here for the first time at the end of the conference. The performance not only won the appreciation of the professional audience, but they themselves joined to the patients and the artists to sing.

This was the start of the SingLung choir. Since then, the choir performs in every year after a week of hard work. New members are added to the ensemble and wonderful, enthusiastic opera singers and dancers complement the choir. They are supported by the help of doctors, nurses and many volunteers and generous supporters.

The main aim of the foundation is to improve the quality of life of patients with respiratory and other chronic diseases affecting the quality of life through cultural, artistic and other recreational activities and to promote their recovery by these activities. The aim of the foundation is also to enable patients to be cared for at a higher level, as well as to improve the working conditions of healers.

Additional aims of the foundation:

  • Purchasing instruments and equipments necessary for the care of patients with respiratory diseases;
  • Improving the treatment conditions of the patients;
  • Organizing and improving the environment of the patients and their healers;
  • Improving communication between the patients and their healers;
  • Preventing burnout of the healers;
  • Maintaining the good mental health of the patients and their healers;
  • Completion of end-of-life care;
  • Better communication of healer-work to the society;
  • Physical and mental improvement of the patient (for the rest of his/her life);
  • Creation of recreational and further training opportunities for the healers;
  • Connecting the patients and their healers with national cultural and social life;
  • Promoting patient mobility.
